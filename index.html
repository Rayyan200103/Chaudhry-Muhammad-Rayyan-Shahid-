About Rayyan

I am an international development and policy professional with a career built at the intersection of multilateral diplomacy, development governance, and analytical research. My institutional journey began at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs Pakistan, where I worked across the UN Division and the Iran and Turkey Division, gaining direct exposure to high-stakes multilateral processes and bilateral diplomatic engagement.

At the UN Division, I contributed to Pakistan’s climate policy representation at COP29, produced four policy briefs on the NCQG and GCF financing mechanisms, and provided strategic and operational support for the SCO Islamabad Summit 2024 — the 23rd Meeting of the Council of Heads of Government of the Shanghai Cooperation Organisation, attended by heads of government from across Eurasia.

At the Iran and Turkey Division, I coordinated the Pakistan-Turkiye 75 Years of Diplomatic Relations Conference, producing five official research reports and diplomatic briefs that directly informed bilateral policy positions, and facilitating engagement with over 50 senior diplomats and government officials at the Foreign Office of Pakistan.

I am currently completing an MA in Global Development at the University of East Anglia, Norwich, where I serve as Course Representative, lead a South Sudan humanitarian simulation as Project Director, and hold the UEA Gold, Silver, and Bronze Awards. I received a Distinction in my Contemporary World Development panel discussion on Chinese aid in Africa.
